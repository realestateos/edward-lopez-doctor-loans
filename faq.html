Frequently Asked Questions About Physician Mortgages

A physician mortgage loan is a specialized home loan program designed specifically for medical professionals. These loans offer unique benefits including 0% down payment options, no private mortgage insurance (PMI), flexible student loan treatment, and the ability to qualify using an employment contract rather than pay stubs. Physician loans recognize that doctors have high earning potential despite significant student loan debt, making homeownership accessible earlier in their careers.

Medical professionals with the following degrees qualify: MD (Doctor of Medicine), DO (Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine), DDS/DMD (Doctor of Dental Surgery/Medicine), DPM (Doctor of Podiatric Medicine), OD (Doctor of Optometry), and DVM (Doctor of Veterinary Medicine). Some lenders also qualify PharmD (Pharmacists). Both practicing physicians and residents/fellows in training programs are eligible. You typically need a credit score of 680+ and a valid employment contract or current employment in your field.

Yes! Medical residents can absolutely qualify for physician mortgage loans in Arizona. In fact, this is one of the primary benefits of doctor home loans. Residents can qualify using their employment contract or letter from their residency program—no pay stubs or lengthy employment history required. This means you can buy a home in your first year of residency with as little as 0% down and no PMI. Edward Lopez has helped numerous residents at Banner, Mayo Clinic Arizona, and other Phoenix-area programs secure financing.

Most physician mortgage programs offer 100% financing with $0 down payment, though some lenders may require 5-10% down depending on the loan amount and your specific situation. The key advantage is that even with 0% down, physician loans do NOT require private mortgage insurance (PMI), which can save you $200-600 per month compared to conventional loans with less than 20% down.

Physician mortgage rates are typically very competitive with conventional loan rates, often within 0.125% to 0.25% of conventional rates. While they may be slightly higher in some cases, the savings from no PMI and 0% down payment usually far outweigh any minimal rate difference. Additionally, Edward Lopez works with 15+ physician loan lenders to shop for the best possible rate for your specific situation.

Unlike conventional mortgages that count your full student loan balance or standard repayment amount against your debt-to-income ratio, physician mortgage lenders use your actual income-driven repayment (IDR) amount—or may exclude deferred student loans entirely. This means having $200,000-400,000 in medical school debt won't prevent you from qualifying, as long as you can document your current payment amount.

Most physician mortgage lenders require a minimum credit score of 680-700. However, some programs accept scores as low as 660, particularly for residents and fellows with strong employment contracts. For the best interest rates and terms, a credit score of 720 or higher is recommended. Edward Lopez works with multiple lenders to find options for various credit profiles.

Most physician mortgage programs are designed for primary residences only. Some lenders may allow second homes or vacation properties, but investment properties typically don't qualify for standard physician loan programs. Required documentation typically includes: (1) Copy of your medical degree or enrollment verification, (2) Employment contract or offer letter (dated within 90 days for residents/fellows), (3) Recent pay stubs if currently employed, (4) Two years tax returns for attending physicians, (5) Two months bank statements, (6) Student loan documentation showing current payment amount, and (7) Photo ID and proof of residency. Additional documents may be required depending on your specific situation.
