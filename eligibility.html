Professional Categories Eligible for Physician Loans

Medical Residents

PGY-1 through PGY-7

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Currently enrolled in ACGME-accredited residency program
  • Valid employment contract or letter from program
  • Medical degree (MD or DO) earned or in progress
  • Credit score of 680+ (700+ preferred)
  • No recent bankruptcies or foreclosures

Arizona Programs Include: Banner University Medical Center, Mayo Clinic Arizona, Phoenix Children's Hospital, Dignity Health, HonorHealth, Valleywise Health

Fellows

Post-Residency Training

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Currently enrolled in fellowship program
  • Fellowship contract or appointment letter
  • Completed residency (MD/DO)
  • Credit score of 700+ preferred
  • Clean credit history

Specialties Include: Cardiology, Oncology, Surgery, Gastroenterology, Pulmonology, Nephrology, Endocrinology, Rheumatology, Infectious Disease

Attending Physicians

Practicing Doctors

Eligibility Requirements:

  • Current employment as attending physician
  • 2 years employment history (or contract if new)
  • Active medical license in good standing
  • Credit score of 720+ for best rates
  • Income verification via W-2s or tax returns

Includes: Primary care, specialists, surgeons, hospitalists, emergency medicine, and all practice settings

Dentists & Specialists

DDS, DMD, DPM, OD, DVM

Eligibility Requirements:

  • DDS or DMD degree (dentists)
  • DPM degree (podiatrists)
  • OD degree (optometrists)
  • DVM degree (veterinarians)
  • Employment contract or current practice
  • Credit score of 680+

Note: Some lenders also qualify PharmD (Pharmacists) with additional requirements

Basic Qualification Requirements

Credit Score Requirements

Most physician mortgage lenders require a minimum credit score of 680-700. However, requirements vary by lender:

  • 680-699: Minimum for most programs, may have slightly higher rates
  • 700-719: Good credit, competitive rates available
  • 720-739: Very good credit, best conventional rates
  • 740+: Excellent credit, premium rates and terms

Edward Lopez works with multiple lenders to find options for various credit profiles, including those with scores in the 660-679 range in some cases.

Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)

One of the major advantages of physician loans is flexible DTI requirements:

  • Physician mortgage lenders typically allow DTI up to 43-50%
  • Conventional loans typically cap DTI at 36-43%
  • Student loans calculated using actual payment (IDR) rather than full balance
  • Deferred student loans may be excluded from DTI calculation

Documentation Checklist

To streamline your physician mortgage application, gather these documents:

📄

Professional Documentation

  • Copy of medical degree or diploma
  • Current CV or resume
  • Medical license (if applicable)
  • Residency/fellowship verification letter
💼

Employment Documents

  • Employment contract or offer letter
  • Recent pay stubs (last 30 days)
  • 2 years W-2s (attendings)
  • 2 years tax returns (self-employed)
🏦

Financial Documents

  • 2 months bank statements
  • Student loan statements
  • Investment account statements
  • 401k/retirement statements
🆔

Personal Identification

  • Driver's license or passport
  • Social Security card
  • Proof of residency/current address
  • Home purchase contract (when ready)

What May Disqualify You

While physician loans are more flexible than conventional mortgages, certain factors may affect your eligibility:

  • Recent Bankruptcy: Most lenders require 2-4 years since discharge
  • Recent Foreclosure: Typically need 3-7 years since foreclosure
  • Active Collections: Unresolved collection accounts may need to be paid
  • Recent Late Payments: Multiple late payments in the past 12 months
  • High Credit Utilization: Maxed out credit cards can lower scores
  • Incomplete Degree: Must have completed degree or be in final year
