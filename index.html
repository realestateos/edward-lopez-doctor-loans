Frequently Asked Questions About Physician Mortgages

Common questions from Arizona medical professionals

Most physician mortgage lenders require a minimum credit score of 680-700. However, some programs accept scores as low as 660, especially for residents and fellows with strong employment contracts. Edward Lopez works with multiple lenders to find the best option for your specific credit profile. The higher your score, the better your interest rate and loan terms.

Yes! Medical residents are eligible for physician mortgage loans using their employment contract or letter from their residency program. You don't need pay stubs or a long employment history. This is one of the biggest advantages of doctor home loans—you can buy a home in your first year of residency with $0 down and no PMI.

Physician mortgage rates are typically very competitive with conventional loans, often within 0.125% to 0.25% of conventional rates. While they may be slightly higher in some cases, the savings from no PMI and 0% down payment usually outweigh any rate difference. Edward Lopez shops multiple lenders to ensure you get the best possible rate.

Most physician mortgage programs are designed for primary residences only. However, some lenders allow second homes or vacation properties. Investment properties generally don't qualify for standard physician loan programs. If you're interested in real estate investment, Edward can discuss conventional investment property loans that may still work with your financial situation.

This is where physician loans excel! Conventional loans use your full student loan balance or standard repayment amount, which can significantly hurt your debt-to-income ratio. Physician mortgage lenders use your income-driven repayment (IDR) amount or may exclude deferred student loans entirely. This means $200K-400K in student debt won't prevent you from qualifying.
