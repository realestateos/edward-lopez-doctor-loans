Understanding Physician Mortgage Loans in Arizona

Physician mortgage loans, also known as doctor home loans, are specialized financing programs designed exclusively for medical professionals. These unique loan products recognize the financial reality of doctors—high earning potential combined with significant student loan debt—providing flexible qualification criteria that conventional mortgages cannot match.

In Arizona's competitive real estate market, from Phoenix's urban core to Scottsdale's luxury communities, having the right financing makes all the difference. How to Qualify for an Arizona Physician Mortgage

Qualification requirements vary by lender and program, but general guidelines include:

Basic Requirements:

  • MD, DO, DDS, DMD, DPM, OD, or DVM degree (or current enrollment in qualifying program)
  • Credit score of 680 or higher (720+ preferred for best rates)
  • Employment contract or letter for residents/fellows; 2-year history for attendings
  • Debt-to-income ratio up to 43-50% depending on lender
  • No recent bankruptcies, foreclosures, or major derogatory credit events

Documentation Needed:

  • Copy of medical degree or enrollment verification
  • Employment contract or offer letter (dated within 90 days)
  • Recent pay stubs (if currently employed)
  • Two years tax returns (attendings)
  • Bank statements (2 months)
  • Student loan documentation showing current payment
  • Photo ID and proof of residency
